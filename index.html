Stability-Constrained Inverse Modeling for ParameterIdentification in Dynamical Systems via Neural Surrogates

Final Project — CSE 598-002 - AI for Science • Fall 2025

Author: Daniel Menacho — daniel@umich.edu

Project Summary

we propose a stability-constrained inverse modeling framework that integrates neural surrogate models with Lyapunov-based stability constraints. Unlike existing approaches that either certify stability for given parameters (forward problem) or learn surrogates without stability guarantees, our method formulates parameter identification as an optimization problem where candidate solutions must simultaneously match desired outputs and satisfy learned Lyapunov conditions.

Our key contributions include:

  • We propose an inverse modeling framework constrained by a learnable Lyapunov function to identify parameter sets that induce stability.
  • We introduce a generalizable framework adaptable to a wide range of neural surrogate models.
  • We validate the reliability of the proposed approach through a proof-of-concept toy-physics experiment, demonstrating its scalability to decision-making tasks for selecting stable parameters during model training.
